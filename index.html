RESEARCH.
Physical AI — Representation & Control
2025 — PRESENT
Independent · Targeting arXiv preprint Sept 2026
Tokenization is the wrong primitive for continuous physical signals. Motor currents, joint torques, thermal fields — these are streams with differential structure, not discrete symbols. Standard pipelines and Euclidean embedding spaces throw that structure away. The alternative I'm building uses Neural CDEs with path signatures as the representation layer and Hamiltonian Neural Networks as the dynamics backbone. The core argument: learned physical controllers need to respect the symplectic geometry of the underlying physics, not approximate it from outside.
Neural CDEs Hamiltonian NNs Path Signatures Physical AI arXiv

2025 — PRESENT
Independent
Gas turbines are a hard test case for physics-informed neural architectures — multi-timescale dynamics, irreversible thermodynamic processes, tight coupling between thermal, mechanical, and fluid domains. Using them as a validation target for the Neural CDE + Hamiltonian architecture. They have the exact failure modes that make physical AI difficult.

Electric Adaptation — Founder
2024 — PRESENT
MIT THINK Scholars Semifinalist · Top ~29 / ~1,000
Founded Electric Adaptation to apply Model Reference Adaptive Control with compound neural networks to AC induction motors. The system self-models electro-thermal-mechanical parameters during operation and uses learned corrections inside Lyapunov-certified stability envelopes. Validated on real hardware across 25+ operating points: 5–8% efficiency gain, 15–20% faster transient response, 15–25% extended thermal margins.
